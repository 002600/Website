Hardware Program

Explore the world of electronics and programming with Arduino.

Course Overview

Our Hardware & Arduino Engineering course immerses students in the world of electronics, coding, prototyping and physical computing. O Over eight weeks, students learn technical skills including reading and creating circuit schematics, building circuits on breadboards, using sensors and actuators, programming Arduino boards in C/C++, debugging hardware and integrating components into functional devices. Each week introduces a new hands-on project, such as a weather station, traffic light system or security sensor, allowing students to apply concepts in practical, tangible ways.

In addition to technical skills, students explore real-world engineering applications, learning how similar technologies are used in industry, how to scale up small projects and how to source materials for larger systems. The course emphasizes interactive learning, including problem-solving challenges, fun facts, short breaks and opportunities to reflect on engineering choices. Students also strengthen teamwork, persistence, analytical reasoning, and creative problem-solving as they troubleshoot, iterate and refine their designs. Students will also engage in discussions of ethical applications in technology, considering safety, responsible design and the social impact of their projects. By the end of the course, students will have built multiple functional projects, gained confidence in electronics and coding, and developed an understanding of how to apply these skills in real-world contexts. Career exploration is woven throughout, helping students discover potential paths in engineering, robotics, and related STEM fields, while reinforcing the importance of systematic thinking, clear documentation, and innovation.
