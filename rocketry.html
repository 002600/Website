Rocketry Program

Explore aerodynamics, propulsion, and flight physics.

Course Overview

Our Rocketry Course introduces middle school students to the exciting world of aerospace engineering through a highly engaging, hands-on learning experience. Over six weeks, students develop technical skills in aerodynamics, propulsion, stability, thrust-to-weight calculations, flight dynamics, and recovery systems. They learn to read design diagrams, evaluate fin configurations, and understand how real rockets are engineered, all while practicing safe construction and launch procedures.

Students will engage in building paper planes, mini rockets, and medium-sized rockets, each constructed collaboratively in small groups. While these rockets are crafted rather than launched, students gain experience in design, assembly, and testing concepts. They will also be introduced to OpenRocket software for flight simulation, helping them visualize rocket trajectories and experiment with design choices in a virtual environment. Students will also engage in discussions of ethical applications in technology, including safety, environmental impact, and responsible engineering practices. The course balances technical skill-building with interactive and engaging elements. Fun facts, problem-solving challenges, discussion breaks, and STEM career spotlights in aerospace keep students motivated and curious. Students also strengthen teamwork, leadership, communication, and critical thinking as they collaborate on design decisions and troubleshoot construction issues in a safe, respectful, and scientifically rigorous environment.

Materials Included Paper, cardboard, and lightweight materials for paper planes and mini sugar rockets Group construction kits for medium rockets Access to OpenRocket flight simulation software Step-by-step building guides and design diagrams Worksheets for calculations, flight predictions, and reflection activities

Course Pace 6 week program 2 classes per week 1 hour per session Each session includes instruction, hands-on construction, interactive challenges, fun fact breaks, and STEM career exploration
