Web Development Program

Design, build, and launch interactive websites

Course Overview

Our Website Development Program introduces middle school students to the fundamentals of building websites from the ground up. Over four weeks, students learn how to structure pages with HTML, style them with CSS, and bring pages to life with JavaScript. Each lesson is taught through CodeSandbox hands-on activities so students can immediately apply what they learn.

The course blends instruction with practice, encouraging students to experiment, debug, and creatively solve real coding problems. Students gain confidence as they progress from simple webpage structures to fully interactive pages. Every session includes fun facts, tech career spotlights, and coding challenges to spark curiosity and expose students to the possibilities within computer science. Students will also engage in discussions of ethical applications in technology, such as privacy, accessibility, and responsible content creation. By the end of the program, students will have built their own mini-websites and interactive features, all while learning how developers think, work, and create. This course is designed to be supportive, fun, and accessible to complete beginners while still pushing students to think critically and explore STEM pathways.

Additionally, mentorship can provide career guidance in technology and STEM, helping students gain insight into professional pathways and build a stronger foundation for future projects and opportunities.
